The following questions were designed to analyze how good and bad characters were introduced and details of those characters.

Where is the data coming from:¶

The data was collected by Walt Hickey, who was the chief culture writer for FiveThirtyEight. This data was first used for his article, "Comic Books Are Still Made By Men, For Men, About Men" in October 2014. The data comes from Marvel Wikia and DC Wikia. The month and year of the first issue each character appeared in was pulled on October 6, 2014. This data also includes information on gender, alignment, identity status, appearances and many other attributes for characters across Marvel and DC universes. For the purpose of this project, the graphs plot specific data for male and female genders. For certain dataframes, there were specific variables omitted to make the data specific and concise. There was also data known as "ND" that was omitted from the graphs for the purpose of data cleaning.

Question 1¶

What is the alignment of the new characters introduced by year?¶

For the opening question, I first wanted to know how many bad and good characters were introduced over the years and to see the trend of each alignment. I started with the .valuecounts() to see how many alignment characters were there. Based on data from the output, I decided to remove ND (No Data) and Reformed Criminals from the dataset. This decision was made because I only wanted to know about bad and good criminals and the numbers for Reformed criminals were very low and almost non-existent when graphed. I included neutral characters because I was surprised to see the number of those characters being introduced to be more than what I expected. The removal of ND and Reformed Criminals data did not affect my graph significantly.

I used pd.crosstab to analyze the data and used the .replace and .drop function to clean the data frame. I chose a line graph because it was the best way to see the data across time and also visually represented the numbers for the data.

years= superfemale

years= years.replace("ND", "Delete")
#years = years.replace("Neutral Characters", "Delete")
years = years.replace("Reformed Criminals", "Delete")
years_new= years.drop(years[years.ALIGN== "Delete"].index)
years_new= years_new.replace("ND", "Delete")
old_years= years_new.drop(years_new[years_new.FIRST_YEAR== "Delete"].index)

prettyyears= pd.crosstab(old_years['FIRST_YEAR'], old_years['ALIGN'])
prettyyears.plot(color=([ "darkolivegreen", "palegreen", "darkseagreen",]))
plt.title("Number of New Characters by Year", fontsize= 30, loc= "center", color= "darkgreen")
plt.xlabel("Years", fontsize = 14)
plt.ylabel("Number of Characters", fontsize = 14)
plt.show()
Analysis¶

The overall trend that I noticed almost immediately was that bad characters were introduced more than good characters. In the graph, you can see that the most amount of bad characters introduced was between 1986-1996. The good characters did follow the same trend as bad characters; however, it was still not as much as the bad characters. It about about 100 characters less than bad characters.
